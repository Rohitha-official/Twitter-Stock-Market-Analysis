Task2.1 T-Test¶

In [19]:
from scipy.stats import ttest_ind
t_stat, p_value = ttest_ind(df['High'],df['Low'])
print("T-Statistic:",t_stat)
print("P-Value:",p_value)
if p_value<0.5:
    print("Reject the Null Hypothesis")
    print("There is a statistically significant difference between High and Low prices.")
else:
    print("Fail to reject the null hypothesis")
    print("There is no statistically significant difference.")

Interpretation¶

The independent t-test was performed to compare the mean High and Low stock prices. If the p-value is less than 0.05, the null hypothesis is rejected, indicating that the average High and Low prices differ significantly. This suggests that the daily trading range is statistically meaningful.

Task2.2 Chi-Square Test¶

In [23]:
from scipy.stats import chi2_contingency
df['High_Category']=(df['High']>
                     df['High'].median()).astype(int)
df['Close_category']=(df['Close']>
                      df['Close'].median()).astype(int)
contingency_table=pd.crosstab(df['High_Category'],df['Close_category'])
chi2,p,dof,expected = chi2_contingency(contingency_table)
print("Chi-Square Statistics:",chi2)
print("P-Value:",p)
if p<0.05:
    print("Reject the null hypothesis")
    print("High Price and Close Price are dependent.")
else:
    print("Fail to reject the null hypothesis")
    print("High Price and Close Price are independent.")

Chi-Square Statistics: 2144.4891323453985
P-Value: 0.0
Reject the null hypothesis
High Price and Close Price are dependent.

Interpretation¶

The Chi-Square test was performed to examine whether High prices and Closing prices are associated. Since the p-value is less than 0.05, the null hypothesis is rejected. This indicates a statistically significant relationship between the High price and the Closing price. Therefore, changes in the High price are strongly associated with changes in the Closing price.
